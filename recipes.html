Coxinha

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups shredded cooked chicken
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 cups chicken broth
  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 cups bread crumbs
  • 3 eggs, beaten
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Oil for frying

Instructions:

  1. In a pan, heat the vegetable oil and sauté the onion and garlic until translucent.
  2. Add the shredded chicken and season with salt and pepper. Mix well.
  3. Pour in the chicken broth and bring it to a boil.
  4. Gradually add the all-purpose flour while stirring continuously until the mixture forms a dough.
  5. Transfer the dough to a clean surface and let it cool slightly.
  6. Take a small portion of the dough and flatten it in your palm.
  7. Place a spoonful of the chicken filling in the center of the dough.
  8. Fold the dough over the filling, shaping it into a teardrop or drumstick shape.
  9. Dip each coxinha in beaten eggs, then roll them in bread crumbs until coated.
  10. In a deep fryer or pot, heat oil to 350°F (175°C) and fry the coxinhas until golden brown.
  11. Remove from the oil and drain on a paper towel.
  12. Serve hot and enjoy!

Feijoada

Ingredients:

  • 500 grams (1 pound) of dried black beans
  • 500 grams (1 pound) of pork ribs
  • 500 grams (1 pound) of carne seca (dried beef)
  • 250 grams (1/2 pound) of smoked sausage (linguiça)
  • 250 grams (1/2 pound) of bacon
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 4 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 2 bay leaves
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Water (enough to cover the beans and meats)
  • Farofa (toasted manioc flour) for serving
  • Rice for serving
  • Orange slices for serving

Instructions:

  1. Rinse the black beans and soak them overnight in water. Drain and set aside.
  2. In a large pot, add the soaked beans and enough water to cover them. Bring to a boil and cook for about 1 hour or until the beans are tender. Remove from heat and set aside.
  3. In a separate pot, add the pork ribs and enough water to cover them. Bring to a boil and cook for 10 minutes. Drain and set aside.
  4. In another pot, add the carne seca and enough water to cover it. Bring to a boil and cook for 10 minutes. Drain and set aside.
  5. In a large skillet, cook the bacon until crispy. Remove the bacon from the skillet and set aside. In the same skillet, add the smoked sausage and cook until browned. Remove and set aside.
  6. In the same skillet, add the chopped onion and minced garlic. Sauté until the onion is translucent.
  7. Add the cooked pork ribs, carne seca, smoked sausage, and bay leaves to the skillet with the sautéed onions and garlic. Cook for a few minutes to combine the flavors.
  8. Transfer the meat mixture to the pot with the cooked black beans. Add enough water to cover the beans and meats. Bring to a boil and reduce the heat to low. Simmer for about 2 hours, stirring occasionally, until the meats are tender and the flavors are well blended. Add salt and pepper to taste.
  9. Remove the bay leaves before serving.
  10. Serve the feijoada hot with steamed rice, farofa, and orange slices on the side.

Pastel

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup warm water
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • Filling of your choice (e.g., ground beef, cheese, ham, hearts of palm, etc.)
  • Vegetable oil for frying

Instructions:

  1. In a mixing bowl, combine the all-purpose flour and salt.
  2. Gradually add the warm water and vegetable oil to the bowl while stirring continuously until a dough forms.
  3. Transfer the dough to a lightly floured surface and knead it for about 5 minutes until it becomes smooth and elastic.
  4. Shape the dough into a ball, cover it with a damp cloth, and let it rest for about 30 minutes.
  5. While the dough is resting, prepare the filling of your choice. You can cook ground beef with onions and spices, or mix cheese with herbs, or any other combination you prefer.
  6. After the dough has rested, divide it into smaller portions and roll each portion into a thin circle or rectangle (approximately 1/8 inch thick).
  7. Place a spoonful of the filling onto one half of the rolled-out dough, leaving a small border around the edges.
  8. Fold the other half of the dough over the filling, pressing the edges together to seal it. You can use a fork to crimp the edges for a decorative touch.
  9. Repeat the process with the remaining dough and filling.
  10. In a deep pan or skillet, heat vegetable oil for frying. The oil should be deep enough to fully submerge the pastels.
  11. Carefully place the pastels into the hot oil, frying them in small batches to avoid overcrowding the pan.
  12. Fry the pastels until they turn golden brown and crispy, turning them over once to ensure even cooking.
  13. Once cooked, remove the pastels from the oil and transfer them to a paper towel-lined plate to drain excess oil.
  14. Serve the pastels hot and enjoy them with your favorite dipping sauce or salsa.

Mortadella Sandwich

Ingredients:

  • 2 slices of bread
  • 4-6 slices of mortadella
  • 1-2 slices of cheese (such as provolone or Swiss)
  • 1 tablespoon mayonnaise
  • 1 tablespoon mustard
  • Lettuce leaves
  • Sliced tomatoes
  • Sliced pickles (optional)
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Spread mayonnaise and mustard evenly on one side of each bread slice.
  2. Layer the mortadella slices on one of the bread slices.
  3. Place the cheese slices on top of the mortadella.
  4. Add lettuce leaves, sliced tomatoes, and pickles (if desired) on top of the cheese.
  5. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  6. Cover the sandwich with the other bread slice, condiment side down.
  7. Press gently to hold the ingredients together.
  8. Cut the sandwich diagonally into two halves or into quarters.
  9. Serve and enjoy!
