Coxinha

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups cooked shredded chicken
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup chicken broth
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup milk
  • 2 cups breadcrumbs
  • Vegetable oil, for frying

Instructions:

  1. Heat the olive oil in a large pan over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and minced garlic, and cook until the onion becomes translucent and fragrant.
  2. Add the shredded chicken to the pan and mix well with the onion and garlic.
  3. Pour in the chicken broth and season with salt. Cook for a few minutes until most of the liquid has evaporated, stirring occasionally. Remove from heat and set aside.
  4. In a separate saucepan, combine the flour and milk. Cook over medium heat, stirring constantly, until the mixture thickens and pulls away from the sides of the pan.
  5. Transfer the dough to a lightly floured surface and knead it for a few minutes until it becomes smooth and elastic.
  6. Take a small portion of the dough and flatten it in your hand. Place a spoonful of the chicken filling in the center and shape the dough into a teardrop-shaped coxinha, enclosing the filling completely. Repeat with the remaining dough and filling.
  7. Roll each coxinha in breadcrumbs until fully coated.
  8. In a deep pan, heat vegetable oil over medium heat. Fry the coxinhas in batches until they turn golden brown, turning them occasionally to ensure even frying. Remove from the oil and place them on a paper towel-lined plate to drain excess oil.
  9. Serve the coxinhas hot and enjoy!

Feijoada

Ingredients:

  • 450 grams dried black beans
  • 1.9 liters water
  • 450 grams smoked ham hocks or pork shoulder
  • 450 grams smoked sausage, sliced
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • 470 grams chopped onions
  • 240 grams chopped celery
  • 240 grams chopped carrots
  • 6 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon ground black pepper
  • 1/4 teaspoon cayenne pepper (optional)
  • 60 grams chopped fresh parsley
  • Salt to taste

Instructions:

  1. Rinse the black beans and place them in a large pot. Add water, cover, and let soak overnight. Drain and rinse the beans before proceeding.
  2. In the same pot, combine the soaked black beans and 1.9 liters of fresh water. Bring to a boil over high heat. Reduce the heat to medium-low, cover, and simmer for 1 hour.
  3. Add the ham hocks or pork shoulder to the pot and continue simmering for an additional hour or until the beans are tender and the meat is cooked.
  4. In a separate skillet, heat the vegetable oil over medium heat. Add the sliced sausage and cook until browned. Remove the sausage from the skillet and set aside.
  5. In the same skillet, sauté the onions, celery, and carrots until the onions are translucent and the vegetables are tender.
  6. Add the minced garlic to the skillet and sauté for an additional minute.
  7. Transfer the cooked sausage, sautéed vegetables, bay leaves, thyme, oregano, black pepper, and cayenne pepper (if using) to the pot of beans and meat.
  8. Continue simmering the feijoada for another 30 minutes, uncovered. Stir occasionally and add water if needed to maintain the desired consistency.
  9. Stir in the chopped parsley and season with salt to taste.
  10. Remove the bay leaves before serving.
  11. Serve the feijoada hot with white rice, collard greens, farofa (toasted cassava flour), and orange slices on the side.

Pastel

Ingredients:

  • 250 grams all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 120 milliliters water
  • 30 milliliters vegetable oil
  • Fillings of your choice (e.g., ground meat, cheese, ham, chicken, shrimp, hearts of palm)
  • Vegetable oil, for frying

Instructions:

  1. In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour and salt. Gradually add the water and vegetable oil while mixing with a fork or your hands until a smooth dough forms. Knead the dough for a few minutes until it becomes elastic.
  2. Cover the dough with a clean kitchen towel and let it rest for 15-20 minutes.
  3. Roll out the dough on a lightly floured surface until it's thin, about 3-4 millimeters thick.
  4. Cut the dough into circles or rectangles, approximately 8-10 centimeters in diameter.
  5. Place a small amount of filling (e.g., ground meat, cheese, ham, chicken, shrimp, hearts of palm) in the center of each piece of dough.
  6. Fold the dough over the filling and press the edges firmly to seal, using a fork to crimp the edges.
  7. In a large skillet or deep fryer, heat vegetable oil over medium-high heat to approximately 175°C (350°F).
  8. Carefully place a few pastels at a time into the hot oil and fry until golden brown, turning once.
  9. Remove the pastels from the oil using a slotted spoon or tongs and place them on a paper towel-lined plate to drain excess oil.
  10. Serve the pastels hot with your favorite dipping sauce, such as hot sauce or tomato salsa.

Mortadella Sandwich

Ingredients:

  • 2 slices of bread (your choice of bread type)
  • 4-6 slices of mortadella
  • 1-2 slices of cheese (such as provolone or mozzarella)
  • Mustard or mayonnaise (optional)
  • Lettuce leaves
  • Sliced tomatoes
  • Sliced pickles (optional)
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Lay the slices of bread on a clean surface.
  2. If desired, spread mustard or mayonnaise on one or both slices of bread.
  3. Place the mortadella slices evenly on one slice of bread.
  4. Layer the cheese slices on top of the mortadella.
  5. Add lettuce leaves, sliced tomatoes, and pickles (if using) on top of the cheese.
  6. Sprinkle with salt and pepper to taste.
  7. Close the sandwich with the other slice of bread.
  8. Cut the sandwich in half if desired, and serve immediately.
